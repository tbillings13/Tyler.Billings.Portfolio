Gettysburg College (Physics)

Capstone Research: Investigating structural changes in surface morphology of (kEV) Proton-irradiated Silicone (PDMS)

Using the Van de Graaf Positive Ion Accelerator, our research team irradiated low energy protons onto a variety of PDMS samples. The samples varied in terms of surface treatments and preparation methods in attempt to further understand the resistivity of PDMS. Applications of this research include circuit boards, satellites, and other aircraft. The mechanical and chemical properties of Silicone make it an essential component of aerospace technology. Our research aimed to further enhance the robustness of silicone when exposed to radiation of particles shown to cause the most damage to silicone in earth's orbit. MATLAB was used to enance the visibility of the damage in our samples through recoloring.

    Experimental Quantum Optics

    In the Optics laboratory at Gettysburg College, our team conducted photon coincidence experiments to observe the wave-particle duality of light. Different phases of our experiments inluded a Helium Neon Laser (155SL 00449) as well as a Two fold diode laser as our photon source. We measured coincidences between multiple detectors to disprove the optical theories of classical mechanics, as well as prove the existence of photons. Our experimentse fully verified the quantum mechanical nature of light that is photons, through down conversion and the manipulation of a Mach Zehnder interferometer. Components of setup included a beam splitter, neutral density filters, polarizers, and Granger detectors. Particle data was extracted using LabView.

    Control of Mobile Robots (Georgia Tech)

    Usisng MATLAB, I implemented the control behavior for a simple, two wheeled mobile robot. The robots decision making was based off sensory data from the five radial sensors. Switching and blending modes used go-to-goal, object avoidance, and follow wall behaviors to steer the robot safely from start to goal. Simulations verified our control behavior's effectiveness in guiding the vehicle efficiently through a variety of obstacle setups using both of our behavioral modes.

    Aerial Robotics (UPenn)

    The course this project was included in focused on the dynamics and trajectories of quadrotors. The objective was to effectively implement PID control into our 3D model in MATLAB, and to generate a minimum snap trajectory from a given set of waypoints. Using the quadrotor's current state and it's derivatives, a piecewise polynomial can be used to generate the desired state along an overall smoother and achievable path. Earlier phases of the project were aimed at integrating 3D PID control into 2D and 1D settings.
