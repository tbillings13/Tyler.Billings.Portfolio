Capstone Research: Vibro-Impact Pipeline Inspection Capsule

Our capsule is designed to increase effectiveness and lower invasiveness of pipeline inspection procedures in challenging pipeline locations and conditions. Utilizing stick slip friction between the capsule and pipe, an internal mass thrusts forward to propel the vehicle, then returns to equilibrium with more gradual recoil, keeping the vehicle below the threshold of static friction (at rest). I conducted a CFD study on the dynamics of the capsule and the effectiveness of the proposed design. I improved previous experiments by adopting the dynamic motion of the prototype into simulations, and improving the concentration and efficiency of the mesh with a developed inlet flow function used to shorten the necessary lead-in length of the modeled pipe. All setups were modelled using Solidworks, capsule motion and velocity profile functions were written in C, and CFD simulations were run on ANSYS Fluent.

  • Tags: CFD, ANSYS Fluent, Solidworks, C

    Solidworks: Hip Implant

    Using Solidworks, all components were dimensioned, designed, and assembled from the online product description of of a modern hip implant. Given dimensions were limited, presenting challenges in scaling and dimension agreement between components.

    Components

  • Socket Liner

    The assembly process featured mostly fixed relationships between the components, with the excetion of the ball-joint sphere and socket liner. The joint was toleranced appropriately to the range of motion of a typical human hip joint. Animations were used to display the (3 DoF) ranges of motion.

    Strain Analysis of Metal Sheet with a hole in the center

    This project was included in the course Mechanics of Materials. In a laboratory setting, we soldered, calibrated, and attached strain gauges to a steel sheet, then applied a (vertical) tension force to the top and bottom of the sheet. The diagram (left) displays the approximate positions of our strain guages as numbered rectangles. We compared our empirical stress and strain data to our calculated predictions and FEA data from ANSYS Workbench.

    CFD Analysis of a Formula 1 Car

    This project was included in my Advanced CFD course on OpenFOAM. We first debugged, and thoroughly examined an intentionally problematic CFD model of a Formula 1 car. Our objective was to further our understanding of CFD and compare the performance of different turbulence models using OpenFOAM CFD. An in depth mesh convergence study was conducted using airflow velocity parameters to assess the degree of refinement necessary near the detailed features of the vehicle. Drag, energy dissipation, pressure, and velocity data were compared across three commonly used turbulence Models.
